title: "The electoral illusion: why voting won't liberate the working class"
date: 2025-05-28
category: "Political Theory"
excerpt: "A TEST. Bourgeois elections offer a choice between management teams, not a path to workers' power. Revolution remains the only answer."
---

Every election cycle, the same spectacle repeats: politicians promise change, the media hypes a “crucial vote”, and after the results, nothing fundamental changes. The currency still devalues, fuel prices still rise, and the working class still struggles.

This is not because politicians are personally corrupt (though many are). It is because the capitalist state, regardless of who occupies its offices, exists to protect private property and capital accumulation.

From a Marxist perspective, elections are a tool of bourgeois democracy – they channel class anger into harmless ballot boxes and legitimate a system that exploits the many for the few.

Revolutionary socialists do not abstain from voting as a matter of principle. But we do not mistake voting for liberation. Real change requires the working class to organise independently, build dual power, and eventually seize the means of production – not wait for a “better” candidate.

The 2025 election cycle will come and go. Our task is to use the period to recruit, educate, and prepare for extra‑parliamentary struggle.

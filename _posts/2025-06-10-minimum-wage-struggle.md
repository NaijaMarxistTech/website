---
title: "Minimum wage and the cost of living crisis"
date: 2025-06-10
category: "Labour Analysis"
excerpt: "A TEST. The current minimum wage falls far below the living wage. Organised labour must demand a radical increase tied to inflation."
---

The national minimum wage was last adjusted two years ago, but inflation has eroded its value by over 35% in real terms. A worker earning the minimum wage today cannot afford rent, transport, and basic food in any major Nigerian city.

This is not an accident. The state and the organised employers have deliberately suppressed wages to attract foreign investment – a race to the bottom that benefits only capital.

The Nigeria Labour Congress has proposed a new minimum wage of ₦150,000 per month. Even that is barely a living wage, but it would provide immediate relief. However, the government has countered with ₦65,000 – a fraction of the demand.

A Marxist analysis shows that wages are not determined by the “needs” of workers but by the balance of class forces. The only way to win a living wage is through mass strike action, not negotiation in good faith with a hostile state.

The working class must prepare for a general strike. Partial measures will not move the bourgeoisie.
